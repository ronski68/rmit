Project Idea

A scheduling assistant for a retail shop

Overview (100)

A website, portal and/or app which would enable a store manager to specify the opening hours, shift times and staffing levels for the store with particular focus on any skill requirements of staff for each shift (Cook, Prep, Shift Manager). Shift would be able to be created and managed graphically by the manager with staff notified of their shifts and able to accept, swap or decline shifts based on their availability.

Motivation (100)

Most retail stores struggle with ensuring that their staff know what shifts they are working and ensuring that the right people with the right skills are scheduled for each shift. A very large amount of the management of any retail store and in particular a food store is spent creating and managing shifts and staff.

Description (500)

A website which will allow owners, managers and shift managers to manage their staff and their shift staffing levels to ensure that there are the right number of people with the right skills allocated to each shift. The website should be able to track the experience of staff and managers, their skills and qualifications and produce reports that can be used either directly or manually to update payroll systems and costings.

Store Managers

The manager of a store would be able to control the opening hours including holidays, staffing hours, shift numbers, shift budgets and shift staffing levels for the store. An overview of the future staffing and costings for each shift would enable balancing of shift costs against anticipated store turnover with the manager able to simply budget using a graphical drag and drop or automated system to create the shifts. If the store manager allows it then the shift managers could be allocated the ability to manage their shifts directly allowing them to do the shift building and budgeting. Store managers would also create staff and promote staff to shift managers, cooks and authorise any skills and qualifications unless they have alloced this to shift managers.

Shift Managers

The manager of a particular shift would be able to drag and drop staffing to ensure a balanced shift taking into account staffing levels, skill requirements and financial budgeting of the shift. The shift manager would finalise their shift and the store manager would then be able to either approve or have the shift automatically approved. When a shift is completed the shift manager may make any notes regarding the shift and possibly add turnover totals to the shift before finalising the shift where the staff experience would be updated and the store manager sent an update of the shift. Shift Managers would be able to mix and match staffing to ensure that not only is there enough staff but also to spread the experience loading. This will ensure there is no accidental all inexperienced cook placed on a shift and that a trainee in one skill is placed with the best experienced staff available.  

Staff

Each staff member would be created by a store of shift manager and then manage their own contact details and information such as holidays, skills, qualifications and availability with their manager able to review and authorise relevant details. The staff member would receive notification of any shifts they have been allocated to and approve or decline the shift based on their availability. The staff members would be able to give up a shift to a similarly skilled and qualified other staff member with the shift manager and or store owner, manager updated about the change and give the right to accept or veto. Staff would be sent push reminders regarding their shift allocation when the shift is finalised and a specific period of time prior to the start of their shift. Staff would be able to make their shift available based on a private one to one offer to their friend or friends or put their shift up for whomever wants it. Maybe on a first come first served or on a manager picks from available bidders option.

 

Tools & Tech (100)

A database stored in the cloud and integrated with a website used as a portal would be the minimum requirements of this system. My preference would be an HTML5 website which could display on a monitor, tablet or mobile with the format being changed to reflect the available real estate. The security would have to be 2 factor with either sms or email used to verify changes made at all levels.

Skills required (100)

Relational database development skills would be necessary with SQL being a leading contender for the database. HTML5 and PHP skills would be the minimum for the web development with probable scripting needed to make the interactivity work.

Outcome (100)

Revolutionising the the currently manual and basically crappy shift and store staffing of retail food businesses would allow free up the time of managers and allow them to focus on the management of their store rather than micro managing their staff. The previous staff history record for a new employee would also ensure that the experience and skills learnt in one store would transfer to a new employee.
